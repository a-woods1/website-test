How do we know that B READY is a successful design?

OVERVIEW

The need for B READY emerged from three months of research with Bloomberg during our Spring semester. Based on the problems that we identified, creating a web app to help both Bloomberg recruiting coordinators and candidates with disabilities handle accommodation requests was important. Candidates with disabilities were not always sure if they should disclose, and recruiters an recruiting coordinators did not always know how to best handle accommodation requests.

However, how could we ensure that whatever we built would successfully address these problems? Our team used further research to both create a framework for measurement and test whether or not we were achieving the right results.

KEY EVALUATION METRICS

Our team decided to measure the success of our designs using the following criteria for each group.

Candidates with Disabilities

Understand
Do I understand what is required for this stage of the interview?

Evaluate
Can I identify what I might need in terms of accommodation for this stage of the interview?

Request
Do I feel confident about requesting an accommodation?

Bloomberg Recruiting Coordinators

Efficiency
How easy is it for me to implement this accommodation request?

Confidence
Do I have everything I need to handle this accommodation?


SUCCESS FOR CANDIDATES

Candidates don't always know if they need to disclose their disability. In order to understand how we could address this, we conducted several co-design sessions with people with disabilities. In one of these sessions, a participant shared a mental model that has served us well for evaluating our prototype. They focus on the following steps when trying to understand if they should request an accommodation:
Understand
What is expected of me?
Evaluate
Will I need an accommodation?
Request
How do I ask for an accommodation?
Our focus became on measuring the key attributes at each stage of this process for candidates with disabilities. Specifically we conducted over 15 usability tests and compared the answers to the following questions based on if a user was using our prototype vs the current resource (an email and PDF from a recruiter):
Understand
What will you be asked to do at this interview?
Evaluate
What accommodations might you need based on this information?
Request
How confident do you feel in requesting this accommodation?
We continued iterating on our designs until the prototype outperformed the email significantly. Some of the changes we made along the way include:

  • Creating accessibility call outs in our content to help users evaluate the need for an accommodation
  • Including an immersive, visual view of key spaces to give candidates more detailed information about a space so they can assess if it will be accessible to them.
  • Create an accommodations menu experience that gives candiates more confidence that Bloomberg has accessibility expertise.
Our current tool for candidates with disabilities gives them a clearer understanding of what is expected in the interview, lets them evaluate what accommodations they might need and gives space for them to confidently ask for an accommodation.
"This would be such a cool tool to have in a job interview. I am always so hesitant to ask for things but this makes me feel way more confident. It would be so nice to have a company say, we get it, there are things that you need and we are okay with providing them. Emailing is something I have always had to do and it is very stressful for me." - Participant

SUCCESS FOR RECRUITING

Similarly, we held several codesign sessions with recruiting coordinators to understand their current processes for handling accommodation requests. What were their pain points? What did they care most about in the experience? Two key points emerged:
"I handle lots of candidates, so I need something that is really quick and easy to use. How can I quickly get through the process even if it is my first time using this."
"I have no idea if we would accommodate a personal aide, or who I would go to about that. I guess I would ask around, or ask my team. I just want to make the candidate experience smooth."
So we decided to focus on two key metrics for recruiters that we measured over ten usability studies:

  • Efficiency: how easy was it to implement an accommodation using this tool?
  • Confidence: how confident are you that you've done everything you need to make the candidate's interview go smoothly?
Several design iterations helped us achieve an optimal level of efficiency while also giving recruiting coordinators peace of mind:

  • Minimizing the number of steps involved with handling an accommodation request
  • Integrating with existing recruiting coordinator systems and tools
  • Providing self-service resources with more information about accommodations
"Everything is pretty straightforward - it's really easy to use, and I think it has everything I need. I don't feel like there's anything else I would need to do, everything is right here." - Recruiting Coordinator
