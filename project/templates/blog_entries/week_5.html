Accelerating the Pace
WEEK 5

Once you identify your target, all that’s left to do is move toward it.

This week our team was able to do the following:

Identify the target → Take three steps forward → Take one step back

Identify the target

This week, we finalized the concept we will build out by the end of the summer! Our team was able to come to this decision thanks to much of our previous weeks’ work. We selected the need we want to address, and then prioritized specific features that will and won’t work for our key stakeholders. As a team, we then brought our HCI lens into play to determine which technologies would be a good fit for implementation. After some initial disagreement, we realized that getting very specific about use cases and implementation types created a path forward. For example, instead of saying we want immersive experiences, do we actually want an virtual desk for someone to sit at? Or do we want a 360 degree video of a space?

The final concept card our team created to define what we will deliver to our client and people with disabilities

Three Steps Forward

While the concept card was one way of defining what this tool would be for the team, creating prototype screen flows allowed us to visualize how different team members were imagining the concept.

Two different manifestations of our concept

Testing key aspects of the designs with five different people with disabilities this week surfaced some great insights and themes to carry our designs forward.

  1. Content phrasing is cruicial (e.g. limitation vs accommodation vs disability). We will need to invest some time in UX Writing.
  2. The more information, the better. Spatial/visual information is a popular feature, and some of our participants are already using tools like Google Street View to get information on unfamilar environments ahead of time.
  3. Including room for customization is important, as every candidate (those with and without disabilities) will have unique needs.

One Step Back

This week’s pace felt a bit sluggish, and it made us realize that this is due to shifting into a different phase of the project. Previously our work has focused heavily on research and decision making. Now that we are entering a rapid design, build, and learn iterative loop, we’re planning to shake up how we’re working by doing the following three things:

  1. Defining parallel work streams
  2. Conducting more iterative, light usability testing
  3. Building the things we know we'll need early on

Part of this shift will also include experimenting with our meeting cadences and locations. Individual work time is easier said than done when you are sitting in a room with three teammates who may be discussing something else that you find interesting. We hope these changes will have us zooming closer to our target next week!
