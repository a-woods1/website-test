How do you ask a company for something, while trying to convince them to hire you?

THE PROBLEM

This is the dilemma some candidates with disabilities face. Imagine trying to convince a company to hire you, while simultaneously asking them for a favor.

These "favors" aren't really favors though. They are accommodations. Accommodations can be anything a person with a disability might need to complete a task. An American Sign Language interpreter might be an accommodation for a Deaf person during an in person meeting. A #2 pencil could be an accomodation for a person who has difficulty typing on a keyboard.

The Americans with Disabilities Act requires companies to provide "reasonable" accommodations to candidates during the hiring process. However, asking for an accommodation can be easier said than done, especially because candidates with disabilities feel it could open them up to bias during the hiring process.

This project is a look into the current hiring process for people with disabilities and how to improve the experience of requesting an accommodation, so candidates with disabilities can thrive.

After testing out these initial designs, we realized that the best place we could provide more help was when it came to understanding the neccessity factor in their disclosure decision. People with disabilities were not always able to evaluate what they will be asked to do during the hiring process. Unfamiliarity with what is needed can lead to two poor outcomes for people with disabilities:

  1. A person discloses, when they do not actually need to
  2. A person does not disclose, when they actually do need an accommodation to perform
Our team, decided to focus on creating a resource that could successfully provide information in a model that one of our research participants shared, for making the disclosure descision:
Understand
What is expected of me?
Evaluate
For what might I need an accommodation?
Request
How can I easily ask for an accommodation?
We're thrilled to introduce Magic8Ball.
The tool that will allow people with and without disabilities to ensure they have exactly what they need during the hiring process! And that benefits both candidates with disabilities and Bloomberg.

For people with disabilities

  1. I know what to expect in the hiring process
  2. I can make smart decisions about what I need ahead of time
  3. I can confidently and easily request what I need

For Bloomberg

  1. I receive accommodation requests in advance
  2. I have help in learning about accommodations
  3. I have help in implementing any accommodations
