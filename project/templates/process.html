How do we know that B READY is a successful design?

OVERVIEW

The need for B READY emerged from three months of research with our client during our Spring semester. Based on the problems that we identified, creating a web app to help recruiting coordinators and candidates with disabilities handle accommodation requests was important. Candidates with disabilities were not always sure if they should disclose, and recruiters an recruiting coordinators did not always know how to best handle accommodation requests.

However, how could we ensure that whatever we built would successfully address these problems? Our team used further research to both create a framework for measurement and test whether or not we were achieving the right results.

KEY EVALUATION METRICS

Our team decided to measure the success of our designs using the following criteria for each group.

Candidates with Disabilities

Understand
Do I understand what is required for this stage of the interview?

Evaluate
Can I identify what I might need in terms of accommodation for this stage of the interview?

Act
Do I feel confident about requesting an accommodation?

Recruiting Coordinators

Understand
Do I understand what this candidate needs?

Evaluate
What do I have the power to do to implement this request?

Act
Can I easily implement this request?

SUCCESS FOR CANDIDATES

Candidates don't always know if they need to disclose their disability. In order to understand how we could address this, we conducted several co-design sessions with people with disabilities. In one of these sessions, a participant shared a mental model that has served us well for evaluating our prototype. They focus on the following steps when trying to understand if they should request an accommodation:
Understand
What is expected of me?
Evaluate
Will I need an accommodation?
Act
How do I ask for an accommodation?
Our focus became on measuring the key attributes at each stage of this process for candidates with disabilities. Specifically we conducted over 15 usability tests and compared the answers to the following questions based on if a candidate was using our prototype vs the current resource (an email and PDF from a recruiter):
Understand
Do I understand what is required for this stage of the interview?

Current Email and PDF
0/10 could identify at least three pieces of information about the tasks, tools and environment for their upcoming interview

B READY:
8/10 participants could identify at least three pieces of information about the tasks, tools and environment for their upcoming interview
Evaluate
Can I identify what I might need in terms of accommodation for this stage of the interview?

Current Resources
Using narrated videos, static images and floorplans helped some participants identify issues with physical space.

B READY:
However, 360 Photos like the kind in B READY helped candidates point out the most potential accommodations they might need.
Act
Do I feel confident about requesting an accommodation?

Current Email
1/6 participants felt confident requesting an accommodation via email, and it took them an average of 15 minutes.

B READY:
With B READY 5/6 participants felt confident requesting an accommodation, which took only five minutes.
We continued iterating on our designs until the prototype outperformed the email significantly. Some of the changes we made along the way include:

Creating accessibility call outs in our content to help users evaluate the need for an accommodation
Including an immersive, visual view of key spaces to give candidates more detailed information about a space so they can assess if it will be accessible to them
Creating an accommodations menu experience that gives candiates more confidence that our client has accessibility expertise.
Favoring a one column design over a three column design that is more overwhelming for candidates to use
"This would be such a cool tool to have in a job interview. I am always so hesitant to ask for things but this makes me feel way more confident. It would be so nice to have a company say, we get it, there are things that you need and we are okay with providing them. Emailing is something I have always had to do and it is very stressful for me." - Cassie, Wheelchair User

SUCCESS FOR RECRUITING

Similarly, we held several codesign sessions with recruiting coordinators to understand their current processes for handling accommodation requests. What were their pain points? What did they care most about in the experience? Two key points emerged that we decied to measure over fifteen usability studies:
Understand
Do I understand what this candidate needs?

Current State
Coordinators frequently find out about accommodations needed in person right when the candidate needs them

B READY:
By creating confidence for candidates, we anticipate that B READY will give recruiters a much improved buffer time in order to process an accommodation request.
Evaluate
What do I have the power to do to implement this request?

Current State
Coordinators either send 100% of requests to Employee Relations or deal with 100% of them by themselves, depending on their seniority and/or how familiar they are with accessibility.

B READY:
With B READY 8/10 coordinators felt confident they had handled the accommodation request correctly, and 10/10 could identify which accommodations required HR review.
Act
Can I easily implement this request?

Current Resources
Using in person coordination, Email, Spreadsheets and without any protocol it can take a recruiting coordinator 24 steps and 140 minutes to handle three accommodations.

B READY:
With B READY it takes 8 steps and 7 minutes to handle those same three accommodations.
Several design iterations helped us achieve an optimal level of efficiency while also giving recruiting coordinators peace of mind:

Minimizing the number of steps involved with handling an accommodation request
Integrating with existing recruiting coordinator systems and tools
Providing self-service resources with more information about accommodations
Reducing scrolling in favor of more clicks, which feel like less work for recruiting coordinators
"“I would definitely recommend this tool to other coordinators! This will help us to be proactive rather than being reactive. I wish I had known about this earlier." - Recruiting Coordinator
